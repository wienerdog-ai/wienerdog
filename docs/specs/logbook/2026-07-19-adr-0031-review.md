# ADR-0031 Codex review round (2026-07-19)

**First adversarial Codex review of the Proposed ADR-0031 (contract-dense specs
extract each contract into one authoritative reference table). Three findings, all
owner-ACCEPTed and applied in a single revision pass. Every cited fact was re-verified
against the repo before disposition: the round-numbering against the round-7 and round-8
logbooks; the cross-document restatement against `WP-a10-escape-harness` and
`WP-a10-reap-mechanism`; and the template's universal acceptance/verification requirement
against `docs/specs/_TEMPLATE.md`. ADR status stays `Proposed` — the revision sharpens the
decision; it does not change what is decided. No other file touched.**

## Findings and dispositions

- **ADR-R1 (ACCEPT, HIGH) — the single-source-of-truth rule, read across documents,
  collided with ADR-0005.** The ADR cited the A10 settle-matrix as a single-source
  precedent, but `WP-a10-escape-harness` both *references* `WP-a10-reap-mechanism`'s matrix
  AND *restates* the per-exit-path reap operations locally in its Current-state, live-proof,
  and acceptance sections (verified: escape-harness lines 72–85, 109, 149–160, 359–368). That
  local restatement is **required** by ADR-0005's One-Document Rule — a spec must be
  implementable from that spec + CLAUDE.md alone, so the harness must carry the contract it
  needs. So a "never restate; purge all copies" rule applied *across* documents would
  contradict ADR-0005, and the cited precedent did not satisfy a cross-document reading of
  the rule. **Applied:** scoped the decision explicitly to **within a single document** —
  amended the headline ("one source of truth per contract *within that document*") and added
  a **"Scope: within a single document (the cross-document boundary)"** clause. Across
  documents ADR-0005 governs and takes precedence: each spec must carry its own local copy of
  a contract it needs; that copy is required self-containment, not a forbidden restatement.
  Defined the boundary crisply — *forbidden* = the same document restating a contract outside
  its own table; *allowed* = a different document carrying its own local copy per ADR-0005,
  plus in-document worked examples that cite the table. Reframed the A10 example: the genuine
  win is the matrix *within* `WP-a10-reap-mechanism` (its four drifting prose descriptions
  collapsed to one table); `WP-a10-escape-harness` legitimately keeps a local copy because
  ADR-0005 requires it.

- **ADR-R2 (ACCEPT, HIGH) — the "when NOT to tabularize" exception was spec-level and
  self-swallowing.** The exception exempted a *whole spec* merely for having an acceptance
  test or live harness. But `docs/specs/_TEMPLATE.md` requires **every** WP to carry
  acceptance criteria + verification steps, so a spec-level reading would exempt nearly every
  spec and the policy would rarely apply — and the ADR's own successes contradict it: the
  incident-runbook was tabularized *despite* a mandatory three-config end-to-end dry-run gate,
  and the settle-matrix *despite* a live merge-gate harness. **Applied:** narrowed the
  exception from **spec-level to contract-level** — a specific contract is exempt only when a
  runnable gate enforces **every one of its facts exhaustively and directly** (the gate is
  then itself the single source of truth for that contract); a general acceptance test /
  harness does **not** exempt the spec's *other* contracts, because a harness proves runtime
  behavior, not that every scattered prose copy of a path/rule agrees. Made the runbook and
  A10 examples consistent under this framing (tabularized because their contracts were not
  each exhaustively gate-enforced). Kept the honest core: genuinely progressive design work
  is not a tabularization target. Aligned the Consequences cost/risk bullet to the
  contract-level wording.

- **ADR-R3 (ACCEPT, MEDIUM) — the evidence miscounted rounds.** The ADR said the
  core-path-resolution contract was hit across **four separate rounds**, but per the logbooks
  R7-1 was in round 7 and R8-2/R8-3/R8-4 were all in round 8 — **four findings across two
  consecutive rounds**, not four rounds. **Applied:** corrected the Context evidence to "four
  findings across two consecutive rounds (rounds 7 and 8)" and the circuit-breaker rationale
  from "~4 rounds on core-resolution" to "~2 rounds", while keeping the separate true fact
  that the runbook took eight review rounds in aggregate. This **strengthens** the
  circuit-breaker: its trigger is exactly "two consecutive rounds on the same contract
  family", so the round-8 finding is precisely where it would have fired.

## Decision beyond the dispositions

- **None.** All three findings applied exactly as owner-dispositioned. ADR-0031 stays
  **Proposed**; the title and README summary are unchanged (the revision refines scope and
  evidence, not the decision). The follow-up `WP-contract-reference-tables` (template + the
  two agent definitions) still ships only after ratification to Accepted, unchanged.

## Round 2 (2026-07-19) — same two findings recur; restructure instead of re-patch

**Second adversarial Codex round on the same Proposed ADR-0031. It landed on the SAME
two issues as round 1 — R1 (the cited single-source precedent contradicts the "never
restate" rule) and R2 (the "when NOT to tabularize" clause is internally false because it
carries a second, subjective exception). Two consecutive rounds hitting the same thing is
exactly the "stop patching, restructure" signal — so this round was NOT dispositioned
finding-by-finding; the Decision's core was restructured around a sharper framing. ADR
stays `Proposed`. Only the ADR and this logbook were touched; `docs/adr/README.md` and the
title were left unchanged.**

### Round-2 findings (both re-verified against the live specs before acting)

- **R1 (recurs) — the "clean single-source precedent" claim was still false.** The
  round-1 revision reframed the A10 settle-matrix as the genuine within-document win, but
  `WP-a10-reap-mechanism` **still independently re-declares** the per-exit-path reap set
  *outside* its settle-path matrix table: verified in the Deliverables `modify` cells
  (the `run-job.js` / `dream.js` rows), the "unified rule in one line" prose (~lines
  436–455), the wiring bullets (~462–513), and several acceptance criteria (~712–737).
  So the ADR was still holding up a spec that contradicts its own rule as a precedent —
  the exact R11-2 pattern (a table plus surviving normative copies) mid-remediation.

- **R2 (recurs) — the single-exception claim was still internally false.** The round-1
  revision narrowed the "when NOT to tabularize" carve-out to a contract-level
  exhaustive-runnable-gate exception, but then **re-opened** a second, subjective
  "genuinely progressive design work stays prose" exception in the same clause — so "only
  when a runnable gate enforces the contract" was not actually exclusive.

### Root cause (why the round-1 patch did not hold)

Both findings share one root: the core rule was phrased as textual non-repetition ("one
source of truth; never restate; purge every scattered copy"), which is too crude to
separate **dangerous** repetition (an independent *normative* re-declaration that can
silently drift from the authority — the A9/A10 churn, and what R11-2 proved) from
**necessary/harmless** repetition (explanatory prose that cites the authority, and the
cross-document derived copy the One-Document Rule requires). A patch that keeps the crude
frame keeps re-generating the same two contradictions.

### The restructure (applied)

- **Core rule reframed around AUTHORITY, not textual non-repetition.** Each contract has
  exactly one designated authority (its reference table); every other mention must be
  subordinate — either prose that cites the table, or a cross-document derived local copy
  (ADR-0005) marked derived-from it. The violation to eliminate is an **independent
  normative re-declaration** that can drift, **not** explanatory prose that cites the
  authority. "Purge every scattered copy" is re-expressed as "convert every independent
  normative re-statement into a citation." R11-2 re-expressed in these terms: a table plus
  surviving independent normative copies is the self-contradiction to avoid.
- **Examples made honest.** `WP-a9-incident-runbook` is the **clean** precedent (copies
  actually reduced to citations; round 9 returned APPROVE). `WP-a10-reap-mechanism` is now
  the **cautionary / in-progress** case — its settle-matrix is the authority, but the
  surviving out-of-table normative re-declarations are the R11-2 danger mid-remediation;
  the ADR explicitly does **not** claim it is a finished single-source exemplar.
- **Collapsed to ONE exception.** A contract is exempt only when a runnable gate enforces
  every one of its facts exhaustively and directly (the gate is then its authority). The
  "progressive design" leniency was narrowed to **pre-contract design notes that do not yet
  state a discrete contract** — the moment discrete contract facts appear, only the
  exhaustive-gate exception applies. The second, subjective exception is gone.
- **Consistency confirmed.** Rule, both examples, and the single exception no longer
  contradict: the reap-mechanism example is consistent with the rule *as the
  cautionary/partial case* (it need not be pure — and the ADR no longer claims it is).
- Kept intact: the 3+-discrete-contracts trigger, the four-point integration
  (template / wd-architect / wd-reviewer / codex-review circuit-breaker), the
  within-vs-cross-document boundary, the ADR-0029/0005 relationships, and the
  no-daemon/no-telemetry / does-not-change-One-Document-Rule-or-Deliverables consequences.

### Dogfooding note — the ADR's own circuit-breaker fired on the ADR

This ADR *prescribes* a codex-review circuit-breaker: two consecutive rounds with a
finding on the same family → stop finding-by-finding patching and do a structural
(extraction) pass. Round 1 and round 2 produced findings on the **same two issues (R1,
R2)** — two consecutive rounds on the same thing. So the ADR's own two-rounds signal
fired **during its own review**, and it was honored: this round is a restructure of the
core decision, not a third same-shape patch. The mechanism validated itself on itself.

## Round 3 (2026-07-19) — reframe on a battle-tested external model; make the rule satisfiable

**Third pass on the Proposed ADR-0031. Rounds 1 and 2 both failed on the same root cause,
and so did the round-2 restructure when re-read against a real wienerdog spec: the rule was
still phrased as a species of non-restatement ("convert every independent normative
re-statement into a citation; grep-verify none survives"), and that is UNSATISFIABLE given
the mandatory spec shape. Every WP spec is REQUIRED to carry a Deliverables table and
acceptance criteria, and those ARE normative statements of contract facts — so no real spec
can reach "zero independent restatements," and every cited example (even the clean runbook)
still "restates" its contracts in Deliverables/acceptance. The fix is not another patch of
the same frame; it is to adopt a frame that was already proven elsewhere. ADR stays
`Proposed`, date 2026-07-19. Only the ADR and this logbook were touched; `docs/adr/README.md`
and the title were left unchanged.**

### The model adopted (adapted, and made fully self-contained)

Reframed the core around the **"Contract-Dense Task Gate"** proven in the pairflow
project's spec-authoring tooling. Credited in one line in the ADR; the mechanism is
restated in full in-doc so a wienerdog reader needs nothing from pairflow (no path/link
dependency). Three moves, adapted to wienerdog:

- **Canonical source, summaries allowed.** One reference table is a contract's single
  canonical source of truth. Other sections MAY mirror/summarize it, but MUST defer to it —
  the forbidden thing is a **second INDEPENDENT authority** that re-decides the facts and can
  drift, **not** textual repetition. This is the crucial reframe that makes the rule
  satisfiable: wienerdog's mandatory Deliverables table and acceptance criteria become
  LEGITIMATE mirrored summaries that defer to the canonical table, not violations.
- **Mirrored Surface Checklist** (adapted to wienerdog's sections: Deliverables-table cells,
  acceptance criteria, verification commands/greps, Current-state description, operative
  prose steps). Two obligations: (a) a finding that changes a canonical table row updates
  EVERY registered mirror in one pass before hand-back; (b) a NEW mirror found in review is
  ADDED to the checklist rather than rediscovered each round. This is the robust form of the
  R11-2 lesson — R11-2 was precisely an *unregistered* mirror that drifted from its table.
- **Activation trigger** (adopted pairflow's concrete conditions): activate when TWO OR MORE
  of seven conditions hold (API/result shape change; status/result taxonomy change;
  input/output parsing / payload validation / schema-acceptance change; error / fallback /
  timeout / cancellation / precedence / reason-code change; authority-boundary crossing;
  multiple downstream consumers or successor specs; same contract across multiple mirrored
  surfaces). Replaces the vaguer "3+ contracts of five shapes."

### What this fixes (examples restated honestly and consistently)

- **`WP-a9-incident-runbook` = clean precedent** under this model: Tables A–E are the
  canonical sources; the Deliverables table and acceptance criteria are REGISTERED mirrored
  summaries that defer to them. No second independent authority — clean.
- **`WP-a10-reap-mechanism` = cautionary case:** its settle-matrix is canonical, but R11-2
  exposed unregistered mirror copies (Deliverables cells, one-line unified rule, several
  acceptance criteria) that had drifted; the fix R11-2 applied — realign every stray mirror
  with the matrix — is exactly what a Mirrored Surface Checklist enforces up front. Presented
  as a spec *moving toward* the discipline, not a finished exemplar.

Rule, both examples, and the single exception are now mutually consistent AND satisfiable by
a real wienerdog spec: a spec with a canonical contract table whose mandatory Deliverables
and acceptance sections mirror-and-defer to it is compliant, which no real spec could achieve
under the round-2 "zero restatements" frame.

### Kept / adjusted (no scope creep)

- Kept the SINGLE contract-level exception (a contract is exempt only when a runnable gate
  enforces every fact exhaustively and directly — the gate is then its canonical source,
  table redundant); kept "progressive design" narrowed to pre-contract design notes only.
- Kept the within-document scope vs cross-document boundary — across documents the
  One-Document Rule (ADR-0005) requires a local derived copy, which under the new framing is
  a legitimate cross-document mirrored summary that defers to the owning table.
- Four-point integration kept and sharpened: (1) template gains the Contract-reference
  section + Mirrored Surface Checklist + activation trigger; (2) wd-architect owns the
  pattern and maintains the checklist; (3) wd-reviewer detects, now folding in a LIGHT,
  self-contained **Closed-Contract Drift Check** (adapted from pairflow's companion check) —
  a one-paragraph reviewer check that a refinement does not silently reinterpret a settled
  canonical contract or promote a mirror to primary; explicitly NOT built out as a second
  full gate (owner does not want pairflow's other gates adopted now); (4) codex-review
  circuit-breaker kept as the backstop, with the Mirrored Surface Checklist noted as the
  stronger day-to-day mechanism.
- Kept the ADR-0029/0005 relationships and the no-daemon/no-telemetry /
  does-not-change-One-Document-Rule-or-Deliverables consequences.

### Why a reframe, not a fourth patch

Rounds 1, 2, and the round-2 restructure all kept some version of the non-restatement frame
and all collided with the same mandatory-spec-structure wall. The ADR's own circuit-breaker
says: same family across consecutive rounds → stop patching, change the structure. Honored
again — this round swaps the frame itself for one already battle-tested in another project,
rather than re-deriving it from the failures.

## Round 4 (2026-07-19) — two bounded honesty fixes; title matches body, runbook stops over-claiming

**Fourth pass on the Proposed ADR-0031. The round-3 reframe validated the CORE (satisfiable,
no contradiction with ADR-0004/0005/0029), so the core rule, canonical-source model, Mirrored
Surface Checklist, single exception, activation trigger, and four-point integration were left
UNTOUCHED. Two surface honesty defects survived the reframe and were fixed here. ADR stays
`Proposed`, date 2026-07-19, filename unchanged. Touched only the ADR, `docs/adr/README.md`
(its 0031 row mirrors the title — moved with it), and this logbook.**

### Fix 1 — the title still asserted the retracted "never restate" rule

The title read "…prose cites it, never restates it", but the round-3 body explicitly ALLOWS
deferring restatements and calls the old "never restate; purge every copy" phrasing
unsatisfiable. A normative headline contradicting the validated body is the exact self-drift
this ADR is about. **Applied:** rewrote the title to the actual decision — *"A dense
contract's single source of truth is one canonical reference table; registered mirrored
surfaces defer to it and move with it."* Updated the `docs/adr/README.md` 0031 row to the same
wording (that index row is itself a mirrored surface of the title — keeping them aligned is
on-message). Re-verified nothing normative still says "never restate": the only surviving
occurrence is the historical line noting the old phrasing "was literally unsatisfiable", which
is explanatory, not a rule.

### Fix 2 — the runbook was over-claimed as a checklist-compliant clean example

The Examples section presented `WP-a9-incident-runbook`'s Deliverables and acceptance as
"registered mirrored summaries, all mirrors registered … no second authority anywhere." But
the runbook spec carries **no** Mirrored Surface Checklist (verified: the phrase appears
nowhere in `docs/specs/`), and its sections restate the Tables A–E facts without registering
as mirrors or explicitly deferring — because it was extracted **before** this ADR introduced
the checklist concept. **Applied:** reclassified honestly WITHOUT retrofitting the already-Ready
runbook spec —
- presented the runbook as the **table-extraction precedent**: its evidenced win is that
  extracting the five scattered contracts into Tables A–E produced an APPROVE / clean review
  the very next round (round 9). That evidence stands and is untouched.
- stated plainly that the **Mirrored Surface Checklist is a new discipline this ADR
  introduces**, that **no existing spec yet carries it** (the runbook predates it and shows
  only the extraction half), and that the follow-up **`WP-contract-reference-tables` is its
  first application and demonstration**.
- kept `WP-a10-reap-mechanism` as the cautionary case — its R11-2 drift is exactly what the
  checklist prevents.
- added an explicit note that the ADR does **not** need a fully checklist-compliant example
  to be sound: the extraction evidence is the runbook, the drift evidence motivating the
  checklist is R11-2 itself, so the absence of one complete exemplar is intentional.
